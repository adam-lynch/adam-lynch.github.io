Meet the Team: Adam

Hey!
I'm Adam, a recent addition to the Teamwork lineup. I'm twenty three years old, from Cork City and I love the Web!

Up until now

I don't know how or when I was introduced to computing. What I do know though, is that I was amazed. The word "addiction" was mentioned more than once by family members. As well as this, I was always interested in visual design and good at maths. So, nearing the end of secondary school, I didn't know what career I saw for myself exactly but I knew for certain what it would involve. Computer Science at University College Cork was an easy choice.

Right away, I fell in love with web development, just like Donal the year before me. With bias, I spent most of my time on my web-related subjects, freelancing, side projects, and getting some real world experience with some interesting Irish startups as well as at the University itself.

"Plot Your Course", my final year project, allowed students to map out their college path, laying out the modules on offer and the dependencies between them. Selecting modules impacted the availability of others, as prerequisites were not satisfied and so on. Working forward from first year or backward from final year with a goal in mind, students could really see the impact of their decisions.

Once I finished my final exams and having not fancied anything I was approached with in Ireland, I took a job in Belgium. I spent a good fourteen months in Antwerp working on web applications for Amadeus, most known for their GDS (Global Distribution System) / APIs which most airlines tap into.

After deciding to relocate again and join everyone here at Teamwork, it was good to know that some of them were already familiar with my "work" through Ked.

Our Christmas party team photo. I'm in the centre.

Three months in, I am loving it here. Teamwork has proven to be nothing short of a very comfortable environment filled with people who value quality user-centric software and a sense of humour.

I came here to be challenged and to make a difference. I hope to have as much impact on Teamwork, as Teamwork will have on me.
